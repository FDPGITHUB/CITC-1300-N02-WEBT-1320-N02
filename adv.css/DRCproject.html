DRC Project

Desert Rose Cantina

Hometown Hustle

"While residing in my hometown of Dickson, TN, I had the privilege of undertaking a significant project: designing posters showcasing menu items for a newly established restaurant joining the local community. The establishment, known as 'Desert Rose Cantina,' boasted a specialization in TexMex Southern-style cuisine. Among the culinary delights, the tacos stood out as an absolute sensation. Recognizing their prominence, I endeavored to infuse these menu items with distinctive character and allure. Collaborating with fellow creatives and drawing inspiration from the evocative essence of the Southern desert, I crafted a series of 17 captivating posters. Among these, I've selected some personal favorites to share with you. I trust you'll find them as enjoyable as I did creating them."

The Process

"My process for these posters commenced with meticulous sketching in my personal sketchbook, followed by close collaboration with the restaurant owner to align with her vision. Her enthusiasm for the skeletal motifs and desert ambiance guided the direction of the designs. Leveraging my proficiency in Adobe Illustrator, I translated these sketches into precise vectors, ensuring optimal suitability for both print production and potential future apparel endeavors. Additionally, Photoshop played a pivotal role in enhancing graphic coherence and consistency. Despite the month-long duration of this project, the investment of time proved immensely rewarding, yielding a final outcome that exceeded both the owner's and my expectations. This endeavor holds a special place in my portfolio, signifying a pivotal milestone in my design journey and affirming the value of my artistic endeavors in the eyes of discerning clients."
